Adding monetary value to Internet comments

Oct 3, 2024

For the past few years I have been toying with the idea of an idea/thought market—one where ideas (and more generally thoughts as a whole) are somehow assigned some economic value and traded as such. In lieu of that I have recently been thinking about Internet comments and what it would mean to add monetary value to comments on the web.

I'm a huge fan of Hacker News but early on in my career (circa 2016) spent enough time reading comments on the site that it started becoming a problem. I'd end up spending hours of my day debating what people were saying to form opinions of my own. At some point I read a brief comment about FUD on the Internet which helped me focus less time on the comments and more time on the content of the posts themselves. I can't find that particular comment but consider it to have been helpful enough for dealing with content on the Internet in general that it still stands out.

I believe that often money can be seen as a universal language that can be used to bridge gaps between other factors/biases. Internet comments in their current state are sorted based on either upvotes/downvotes on forums (like reddit, Hacker News, or StackOverflow) or just "likes" on social media (like Twitter or Instagram). Adding some universal monetary value to these comments could make it easier to discern and qualify good and useful user input from bad and useless user input. The currency could take just about any form—standard USD or some Internet currency (like cryptocurrency or some browser metric or custom Chrome extension value) or anything else. I don't know how money/currency creation would work here but I'm sure it's not that difficult a problem, especially if bidders are using standard USD to vote on content.

It would also give lieu to some sort of Internet comment database to serve collections or dumps of comments from across the web. This would work by sorting the most valuable user comments for some time period and then showcasing and classifying interesting content. I think it would be an awesome addition to the Internet.
